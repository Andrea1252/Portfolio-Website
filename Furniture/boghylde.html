Boghylde

Braceless Cantilever Chair

Boghylde (Danish for "Bookshelf") is a braceless cantilevered reading chair developed as an engineering challenge where the structure serves as the aesthetic. The cantilevered posture is tuned for focus, offering enough ease to read while staying composed and attentive.

Category Furniture

Date 2025

Duration 4 Weeks

Skills Machining, Woodworking, Structural Analysis, Rhino

Sculptural in Presence

Cantilever Chair that blends sculpture and function, light yet bold enough for a gallery.

Purposeful in Use

Underseat offers space for curated texts and readings tied to the exhibition.

Structural Analysis

Static structural FEA (von Mises) with a 3000 N distributed seat load (≈675 lbf, ≈306 kg-equiv) mapped stress concentrations across the frame and joints. Minimum safety factor: 1.99 (target 2.146, yield-based) at a localized hotspot, guiding reinforcement of the critical joint region.

Process

Fabrication relied on high-precision machining and 3D-printed jigs to guide complex angle cuts and drilling, ensuring structural integrity without visible bracing. The cantilevered form is supported by a milled steel frame with press-fitted joints.
